Real Racing 3 adds new circuits in update

Date: June 27, 2025

Despite launching way back in 2013, FireMonkeys still supports Real Racing 3. Whereas Forza Street was recently delisted, the mobile racing game is still proving popular. The latest Real Racing 3 update has rolled out, adding three new cars, a revised track, and the start of Round 9.

Launching on 21 June, one of the highlights in Round 9 is the Aston Martin Valhalla Concept. Appearing in the latest James Bond film No Time to Die as well as Rocket League, the Valhalla Concept previews Ferrari’s upcoming mid-engined Ferrari fighter. Sitting below the flagship Valkyrie, the original Valhalla concept was powered by a twin-turbo 3.0-litre V6, but the final production model will utilise a turbocharged 4.0-litre V8 sourced from AMG when it arrives in 2023.

In Real Racing 3, you can earn the Valhalla supercar by completing the five-day Victory or Valhalla event starting on 21 June.

Joining the Valhalla Concept is the Chevrolet Corvette C8 Z06. A track-focused version of the standard Stingray, under the bonnet lies a 5.5-litre naturally aspirated flat-plane crank V8 producing 670 horsepower. That makes it the most powerful naturally-aspirated V8 in history. To unlock the Chevrolet C8 Z06 Limited Series, you’ll have to complete the seven-day Chevrolet C8 Z06 Limited Series starting on 25 July.

Last but not least, the Chevrolet Camaro ZL1 1LE is the Grand Prix for completing Round 9 starting on 21 June. This track-focused muscle car boasts an enhanced aero kit with a gargantuan carbon-fibre rear wing and packs a beefy supercharged 6.2-litre V8 producing 650 hp.

Coinciding with the 2022 New York City E-Prix Special next month, Real Racing 3 update 10.5 includes a revised version of the Formula E New York Circuit. New corners added in the updated street circuit layout offer new challenges for returning players.
